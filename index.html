Intro on Powers and exponents

  1. If a is any number and n is an integer, then a^n = a*a*a........ multiplied n times, where a is base, n the exponent of a^n is read
    as "a to the power of n".

  2. Power 2 = SQUARE and Power 3 = CUBE ,i.e a^2 is read as "a square", a^3 is read as "a cube".

  3. Power of (-1)
    (i) (-1)^ 2 = 1
    (ii) (-1) ^ 3 = -1
    If -1 is raised to the power of an even number its result is 1, Else its result is -1

  4. Laws of Exponents
    Law 1: a^m x a^n = a^m+n
    Law 2: a^m / a^n = a^m-n
    Law 3: a^0 = 1
    Law 4: a^-n = 1/a^n

  5. Squares
    (i) If a number is multiplied by itself, the product so obtained is called the square of that number.
    (ii) Squares of even numbers are always even, Squares of odd numbers are always odd
    (iii) Perfect Squares : 1,4,9,16,25,36 are squares of natural numbers 1,2,3,4,5,6 respectively, Such numbers are called perfect squares.
    (iv) The units place in square numbers is not occupied by the digit 2,3,7 or 8.
    (v) The number of zeroes at the end of a square number is double the number of zeroes at the end of a given number, i.e., the number of zeroes at the end of a perfect square is always even.

  6. Square roots
    The square root of a possitive integer n is an integer whose square is n.
    For example: 4 is the square of 2, 2 is the square root of 4

  7. Cubes
    (i) The number obtained on multiplying a given number by itself three times is called the cube of the given number e.g., 6^3 = 6*6*6 = 216;

  8. Cube root
    (i) Cube root of a number is that number when multiplied itself three times gives the original number. Example: 3 is the cube root of 216.
